## Project Overview

Wayfinding for a 12-acre cultural campus comprising a museum, theater, library, and public gardens. The system needed to serve 500k+ annual visitors speaking 20+ languages.

## Scope

- **Exterior:** 45 directional totems, 12 building identifiers, 8 parking guides
- **Interior:** 200+ room signs, 30 directory maps, 15 regulatory signs
- **Digital:** Interactive kiosk UI, mobile web app integration
- **Pictograms:** 40 custom icons for programs, amenities, accessibility

## Design Decisions

**Typography:** Instrument Sans for its exceptional legibility at distance and small sizes. Custom arrow suite drawn to match the typeface's stroke geometry.

**Materials:** Brushed brass (primary), smoked oak (secondary), matte black aluminum (accents). All materials specified for exterior durability (10+ year lifespan).

**Color:** Warm neutral palette with a single accent (oxide copper) for emergency/accessibility elements. No color-coding by building — relies on form and typography.

## Accessibility

- All signs meet ADA 2010 Standards
- Tactile lettering and Grade 2 Braille on all room signs
- High contrast ratios (7:1 minimum)
- Mounting heights per ADA reach ranges
- Multilingual support via pictograms + QR codes to translated content
